Hello, there!

Welcome to my design portfolio! Here, I am showcasing the works that I have created as part of our Computation in Design module in Semester 2. This module has been a valuable learning experience
for me as it has allowed me to explore the world of design communication through the lens of code
and computation.

Workshops

Throughout the semester, I participated in four diverse workshops, including Creative Coding, Projection Mapping, Making, and Physical Computing. These workshops taught us how to create physical mediums with abstract patterns, expand our knowledge of showcasing videos using code, combine digital tools and physical labor to create unique geometric shapes, and experiment with simple input and output mechanisms utilizing electronics for tangible designs.

In this portfolio, you will find the results of these workshops, including process documentation and deliverables created individually or in small teams. These experiences have deepened my understanding of the intersection of design and technology, and how they can create unique and compelling visual experiences in design communication.

Coding, Screens, Making or Play.

Coding

Andreas Schlegel's Coding workshop explores the creative application of code in design. The brief my partner and I chose was the Axidraw brief where we learned how to create a machine-drawn pattern generated by us. The workshop introduced us to digital techniques that can be applied creatively, expanding our skill set beyond the traditional design toolkit. By selecting a brief aligned with our interests, we explored the possibilities of creative coding in design and gained practical skills for future projects.

For the Coding workshop, we decided to challenge the notion of nature as beautiful and created visuals resembling worms and intestines using circular shapes in p5js. While the coding process was enjoyable, we faced difficulties working with the new Axidraw machine. Nevertheless, seeing the machine draw our code with such precision was fascinating. Overall, this workshop allowed us to experiment with unconventional design ideas and provided an opportunity to explore the capabilities of digital tools in bringing our ideas to life.

Tools used: p5.js, Inkscape, A3 Black paper, A3 frosted paper, metallic markers, black uniball marker

Making

In Dhiya's workshop, we explored the process of transforming code to objects, known as 'Code to Thing'. Through this workshop, we were introduced to a code template to generate 3D objects. These 3D objects were then converted into templates using blender, which allowed us to apply add-ons to physically create a 3D object. Additionally, we focused on documenting our work and generating content for the 3D objects, resulting in an A3 folded printed artefact that could function as a brochure or poster. Through these processes, we gained an understanding of working with both digital and physical tools, as well as a workflow from digital to print.

The process of creating a 3D object from code was both exciting and challenging. As I experimented with different values, I realized how small changes could have a big impact on the shape of the object. However, the most challenging part was cutting and gluing the shape as it required lots of precision, which I struggled with. Dhiya taught us that using the sharp corner of the penknife blade to score the paper improves accuracy, which was so helpful. The process also made me aware of the importance of meticulousness when gluing, as even small smudges can affect the final outcome. (Either way, I'm still proud of how my craftmanship skills improved through this project. It was waaay worse before.)

Tools used: Craft Knife, glue tape, spray mount, Ruler

Screens

In Jo's Projection Mapping workshop, we explored the basics of projection mapping and developed a concept for the prompt "Other Worlds" using p5js. Our project involved projecting static and moving images onto physical shapes to create an immersive visual experience that reflects otherworldly concepts. During the open studio, we carefully selected project elements that complemented each other, allowing for a seamless integration of the visuals and physical objects. Overall, this workshop helped us understand the potential of projection mapping as a tool to enhance visual communication and create impactful designs.

My partner and I focused on creating surreal/absurd concepts of a fish swimming in unusual environments. We used a 3D hexagonal shape and a triangle as our medium. While it was exciting to see our concept come to life, we faced several roadblocks. The masking required lots of focus, and even a slight shift in angle could throw off the entire projection. Looking back, we could have had a more experimental approach and pushed the boundaries further. However, this experience was a valuable lesson in learning how to balance creativity with practicality, and I look forward to applying these lessons in future projects.

Tools used: Craft Knife, glue tape, ruler

Play

In this workshop, we explored the intersection of the physical and digital worlds through the use of basic electronics and an Arduino microcontroller. Our main objective was to create an interactive artifact that narrates a story that loosely revolves around the theme of sentient shapes, using electronics and a controller. By interacting with the artifact, the user can trigger abstract expressions on a digital screen or initiate physical feedback through light or motion in the real world. Besides gaining knowledge of a new medium, this workshop also encouraged us to communicate our ideas using non-traditional media, which allowed us to engage in cross-disciplinary discussions and expand our skill set.

We teamed up in duos and focused on physical to digital outputs. However, we were unsatisfied with our initial idea and decided to pivot at the last minute. Our new concept aligned better with the slightly abstract brief and involved coding a visual that mimicked disco lights. The presence of light would cause the disco ball to spin more rapidly, while the absence of light would result in a slower spin and more vibrant lights. Ultimately, this project encouraged us to be open to experimentation, and we successfully incorporated both physical and digital elements into our creation. It also highlighted the importance of relevance, as our disco-themed artefact was best enjoyed in the darkness of night.

Tools used: Corrugated plastic, ruler, craft tape

Conclusion

What have I learned over the course of this semester?
This semester has been a little bit of an eye-opener for me as it became more evident how Computation connects closely to Design Communication. I also learned that computation is not just a tool for making flashy graphics or interactive websites; it's a mindset that can help you think more critically and creatively about the design process. By breaking down complex problems into smaller, more manageable chunks, you can explore different possibilities and experiment with new ideas that you might not have considered otherwise. Very cool..

Some of my most interesting findings this semester are?
My personal favourite would be discovering how the Axidraw can effortlessly bring even the most meticulous generative art to life. It was extremely impressive to see how the Axidraw brought the most elaborate ideas to life with such accuracy and attention to detail. It helped me realise how smoothly technology and creativity can coexist and the seemingly endless possibilities that result.

What are some of my achievements?
Opening my mind and broadening my approach to design. Before this module, I would say that my design approach was a bit narrow, which often resulted in limited ideas and solutions. However, through the various workshops and projects, I learned to think more abstractly and generate unique ideas that incorporated code. I discovered that computation can be a powerful tool to push the boundaries of design and unlock new possibilities. By embracing new techniques and experimenting with different mediums, I found myself able to break out of my comfort zone and create designs that were truly original and captivating.
